Detailed Explanations

Question 1

Correct Answer: B (Detail)

Explanation: The three commonly used measures to assess threat intelligence are Timeliness, Accuracy, and Relevance (TAR). "Detail" is NOT one of the standard assessment criteria.

Question 2

Correct Answer: C (Political/philosophical beliefs)

Explanation: Hacktivists are motivated by political or philosophical beliefs. They use hacking as activism to promote ideological causes.

Question 3

Correct Answer: A (Script kiddie)

Explanation: Script kiddies use existing tools and scripts, lacking advanced skills or resources.

Question 4

Correct Answer: A (Advanced Persistent Threat)

Explanation: APTs are highly skilled, well-funded, and persistent threat actors, often linked to nation-states.

Question 5

Correct Answer: D (ISACs)

Explanation: Information Sharing and Analysis Centers (ISACs) facilitate sharing threat intelligence among organizations.

Question 6

Correct Answer: A (Funding)

Explanation: Funding is a key resource for threat actors, enabling them to acquire tools and expertise.

Question 7

Correct Answer: A (Phishing)

Explanation: Phishing is a common threat vector used to gain unauthorized access or information.

Question 8

Correct Answer: D (Anonymous)

Explanation: Anonymous is a well-known hacktivist group, recognized for its decentralized structure and activism.

Question 9

Correct Answer: A (Already has access)

Explanation: Insider threats are dangerous because they already have authorized access to systems and data.

Question 10

Correct Answer: D (Supply chain)

Explanation: Supply chain attacks target vulnerabilities in third-party vendors or service providers.

Question 11

Correct Answer: A (Software updates)

Explanation: Compromised software updates are a common supply chain attack vector.

Question 12

Correct Answer: B (Threat feeds)

Explanation: Threat feeds provide up-to-date information on emerging threats and vulnerabilities.

Question 13

Correct Answer: C (Shared responsibility)

Explanation: Cloud security is a shared responsibility between the provider and the customer.

Question 14

Correct Answer: A (SIEM)

Explanation: Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) tools aggregate and analyze security data.

Question 15

Correct Answer: B (Indicators of Compromise)

Explanation: IoCs are artifacts observed on a network or in an operating system that indicate a potential intrusion.

Question 16

Correct Answer: A (Shadow IT)

Explanation: Shadow IT refers to systems and solutions built and used inside organizations without explicit organizational approval.

Question 17

Correct Answer: A (Vendor management)

Explanation: Managing vendor relationships is critical to reducing supply chain risks.

Question 18

Correct Answer: C (OSINT)

Explanation: Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) is gathered from publicly available sources.

Question 19

Correct Answer: A, C (Nation-state, Organized crime)

Explanation: Both nation-state actors and organized crime groups are highly resourced and pose significant threats.

Question 20

Correct Answer: D (Hacktivism)

Explanation: Hacktivism is hacking for a political or social cause, not for financial gain.
