1 Correct Answer: B (Detail) Easy

✓ Why B is Correct:

The three commonly used measures to assess threat intelligence are Timeliness, Accuracy, and Relevance (TAR). "Detail" is NOT one of the standard assessment criteria. While detail can be important, it's not a primary assessment measure.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • A (Timeliness): This IS a key measure - threat intelligence must be current to be useful
  • C (Accuracy): This IS a key measure - incorrect intelligence is worse than no intelligence
  • D (Relevance): This IS a key measure - intelligence must apply to your environment

💡 Exam Tip:

Remember "TAR" - Timeliness, Accuracy, Relevance. These are the three pillars of threat intelligence assessment.

2 Correct Answer: C (Political/philosophical beliefs) Easy

✓ Why C is Correct:

Hacktivists are motivated by political or philosophical beliefs. They use hacking as a form of activism to promote their ideological causes, expose perceived injustices, or advance political agendas. Examples include Anonymous targeting organizations they disagree with philosophically.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • A (War): This is typically associated with nation-state actors, not hacktivists
  • B (Financial gain): This motivates organized crime, not hacktivists who are ideologically driven
  • D (Ethical): This describes white-hat hackers doing authorized security testing

📚 Concept Review:

Hacktivists = Hacking + Activism. They believe they're fighting for a cause, even if their methods are illegal.

3 Correct Answer: A (Authorized attacker) Easy

✓ Why A is Correct:

Kolin is an authorized attacker (white-hat hacker). His company was hired to conduct penetration testing, meaning he has explicit permission and authorization to test the security of the health-care system. This is legitimate, legal security testing.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • B (Unauthorized): He has authorization through a contract
  • C (Unknown): Not a standard classification for threat actors
  • D (Semi-authorized): He's fully authorized, not partially
4 Correct Answer: A (Nation-state actor) Easy

✓ Why A is Correct:

Nation-state actors are most commonly associated with APTs (Advanced Persistent Threats). They have the resources, sophistication, and motivation to conduct long-term, stealthy campaigns. Examples include Chinese military units conducting cyber espionage.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • B (Hacktivist): Usually not persistent enough for APT classification
  • C (Unskilled): Lacks the sophistication for APT attacks
  • D (Insider): Typically not associated with APT campaigns

📚 APT Characteristics:

  • Advanced techniques (often zero-days)
  • Persistent (months or years)
  • Well-funded (usually government-backed)
5 Correct Answer: D (ISACs) Medium

✓ Why D is Correct:

ISACs (Information Sharing and Analysis Centers) were established following Presidential Decision Directive-63 in 1998. They facilitate sector-specific threat information sharing (e.g., Financial Services ISAC, Healthcare ISAC).

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • A (DHS): Department of Homeland Security oversees but didn't create ISACs
  • B (SANS): Private training organization, not government-created
  • C (CERTs): Computer Emergency Response Teams serve different purposes
6 Correct Answer: A (Nation-state actors) Easy

✓ Why A is Correct:

Nation-state actors have virtually unlimited resources backed by government funding. They can afford extensive research, zero-day development, and long-term operations.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • B (Organized crime): Well-funded but not to nation-state levels
  • C (Hacktivists): Usually limited resources, volunteer-based
  • D (Insider threats): Limited to personal resources
7 Correct Answer: A (Email) Easy

✓ Why A is Correct:

Email is the most commonly exploited vector for remote attackers. Phishing, malware attachments, and business email compromise can all be executed from anywhere in the world.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • B (Direct access): Requires physical presence
  • C (Wireless): Requires proximity (parking lot)
  • D (Removable media): Requires physical distribution

✓ Why D is Correct:

Anonymous is the most well-known hacktivist group, operating as a decentralized collective pursuing various ideological causes.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • A (Chinese military): Nation-state actor, not hacktivist
  • B (U.S. government): Nation-state actor, not hacktivist

9 Correct Answer: A (Behavioral) Medium

✓ Why A is Correct:

Behavioral assessments help identify insider threats by monitoring unusual patterns like accessing files outside normal duties, working odd hours, or exhibiting disgruntlement.

Why Other Options Are Wrong:

  • B (Instinctual): Not a formal assessment type
  • C (Habitual): Not a security assessment method
  • D (IoCs): Used for external threat detection, not insider behavior

20 Correct Answer: D (Unauthorized hacking) Hard

✓ Why D is Correct:

Despite good intentions and reporting the vulnerability, Renee accessed systems without permission, making this unauthorized hacking. Good intentions don't make gray-hat hacking legal.

⚠️ Critical Point:

Authorization is binary - you either have it or you don't. Good intentions don't provide legal authorization.

Common Exam Patterns

Pattern Recognition Tips:

  • Financial motivation → Think Organized Crime
  • Political motivation → Think Hacktivist
  • Long-term + Sophisticated → Think APT/Nation-state
  • Unauthorized but well-intentioned → Still illegal (Gray-hat)
  • Employee using personal tools → Shadow IT
  • Hardware tampering before delivery → Supply chain attack
